Welcome to Hacktoberfest!

We're super excited to have you be attending Hacktoberfest this Saturday, October 17th! With continuous events happening from 9am - 9pm (come and go of course) here's all the details to get you off to a great start!

Before we talk about the events and schedule make sure that you have joined the ACM Discord Server @ acmutd.co/discord. All our event announcements will happen on there so it's important to join (and as a special bonus you'll get a special color to highlight your name).

Make sure to always check our website and review our welcome packet for details --> hack.acmutd.co/welcome

Schedule

With events happening all day it can seem a little tricky to decide where to start off. Our full schedule can be found on our website https://hacktoberfest.acmutd.co but we recommend making sure to attend our Kickoff happening at 10am CST. You'll have a chance to hear about the event, take an inside look into open source & listen from our amazing Keynote Speaker! Building up to the Kickoff we have a special guest coming in to talk about Entrepreneurship. Learn more about her on the website & join her talk here.

During the event you will have the option to participate in 3 different tracks. Firstly, an ACM Track focused on workshops, guest speakers & lightning talks. Whether you're looking to build your own API or listen to tips from industry veterans we've got you covered. Next we have a Student Org Track where we have 12 of the best organizations from the ECS community at UT Dallas present talks & panels about topics ranging from Cloud Computing to Decentralized Finance. Finally we have the Community Track filled with events to help you take a break from the rush of everything else happening during the day. Chill out solving mysteries and maybe play some Minecraft to pass the time. 

Making the best out of Hacktoberfest!
  • Sign up on the Digital Ocean (company sponsoring the swag) website as well in order to get that free T-shirt!
  • Attend the ACM workshops to contribute to open source projects and make those 4 pull requests to be eligible for swag!
  • Go to as many events as possible - the more events you attend, the more you’re bound to learn, plus you’ll be higher up on the leaderboard and might even earn some prizes!
  • If you’re on the lookout for some CS organizations, make sure to attend the Organization fair (all major CS organizations will be there) as well as events hosted by particular student orgs at UTD. 
  • Network with the guest speakers and gain some valuable advice.
  • Try to participate in as many events with prizes as you can, this increases your chances of winning. 
  • Attend community events which are great opportunities to make friends who have common interests.
Looking forward to seeing you at ACM Hacktoberfest!
